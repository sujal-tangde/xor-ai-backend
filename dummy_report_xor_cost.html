Architecture Analysis

The electronics partition into five functional blocks on a single 4-layer main board, with user controls broken out onto a flex circuit. The architecture is a conventional low-cost integrated BT-audio topology: a single SoC handling the radio, codec and DSP, feeding a discrete Class-D stage, with a separate linear-charging and protection front end for the cell.

Wireless + DSP
Beken BK3266 BT 5.1 SoC — integrates radio, Bluetooth Classic/BLE stack, audio codec and DSP. Single-chip approach removes a discrete codec, the primary cost lever in this class.
Audio output
Nsiway NS4165 mono Class-D amplifier, ~3 W into 4 Ω. Filterless output stage driving the 45 mm driver directly.
Power & charging
Injoinic IP2312 USB-C linear charger + DW01A/FS8205 protection (BMS) for the single 18650 cell. No boost converter — SoC and amp run directly from cell voltage.
User interface
Button/LED flex circuit (FPC) with 4 tactile switches and an RGB status LED, ZIF-connected to the main board.
Board & interconnect
70 × 42 mm, 4-layer FR-4, ENIG finish, double-sided SMT. 186 placements, 31 unique parts, 6 through-hole points (cell tabs, driver, USB shell).
Cost-driver insight: The single-SoC + filterless Class-D topology is what keeps active-silicon cost under ₹250 / unit. A discrete codec + separate MCU design would add an estimated ₹140–190 / unit and a second crystal — confirming this is a cost-optimized reference design.

Sourcing & supply observations

  • Concentration risk: SoC, amp and charger are single-sourced from Chinese fabless vendors (Beken, Nsiway, Injoinic) — all available via LCSC, but with limited Western distribution. A second-source plan is advisable above 10k volume.
  • Duty exposure: Active silicon enters at 0% BCD, but the cell (15%) and driver (10%) drive the duty load. Domestic cell sourcing could remove ~₹35 / unit.
  • Price volatility: The 18650 cell is the most volatile line — lithium spot movement of ±15% shifts unit cost by ~₹40. Pricing is timestamped at generation.
  • Lead time: Longest-lead item is the BT SoC at 8–10 weeks; all others < 4 weeks.
